# Review Intake Worksheet

Use this worksheet before asking your AI assistant to analyze documents with Tracewright.

The quality of a Tracewright review depends on the source material, the AI model, the prompt, the user's instructions, and the reviewer's domain knowledge. Do not upload private, sensitive, or third-party materials to an AI system unless you are allowed to do so and you understand that system's privacy terms.

## 1. Choose a Review Mode

Pick the closest mode. If more than one applies, tell the AI which one is primary and which one is secondary.

### A. Claim and Fact Consistency

Use when you want to check whether statements, dates, descriptions, citations, methods, or public claims are internally consistent and sufficiently supported.

Best for:

- public narratives
- institutional statements
- dossiers
- historical records
- factual articles
- provenance files

Ask the AI to emphasize:

- factual claims
- chronology
- source roles
- missing evidence
- contradictions
- next verification steps

### B. Academic Review

Use when you want to improve or review an academic paper, essay, research proposal, or scholarly argument.

Ask the AI to emphasize:

- thesis and argument structure
- method and sample description
- citation support
- causal language
- overgeneralization
- limitations
- unsupported claims
- what a human reviewer should check before publication or submission

### C. Correspondence / Personal Narrative Continuity

Use when reviewing a sequence of personal, conversational, or relational writing.

Ask the AI to emphasize:

- response alignment
- continuity of memory and claims
- changes in tone or relationship stage
- concrete callbacks
- style drift
- disclosed translation or AI assistance

Important: this mode should not be used to accuse, shame, or expose a person for suspected AI use.

### D. Authorship / Mediation Workflow

Use when you want to understand how a text surface may have been shaped.

Ask the AI to emphasize:

- translation
- AI drafting
- AI polishing
- human editing
- copied or templated sections
- source-provided detail versus generated connective prose
- disclosed or known workflow

Important: this mode should not produce a binary "AI or human" verdict.

### E. Provenance / Source-Role Review

Use when documents come from different authors, institutions, time periods, or source roles.

Best for:

- art provenance
- archives
- public-figure dossiers
- legal or policy source packets
- institutional histories
- mixed public/private records

Ask the AI to emphasize:

- who said what
- in what role
- at what date
- based on what evidence
- what is primary, secondary, reported, interpreted, or missing

## 2. State the Review Purpose

Complete this sentence:

```text
I want to use Tracewright to understand whether...
```

Examples:

```text
I want to understand whether this article's claims are well supported before I cite it.
```

```text
I want to understand whether this public narrative changed over time, and which sources support each version.
```

```text
I want to understand whether this academic draft has weak claims, missing limitations, or overgeneralized conclusions.
```

## 3. Define What the AI Must Not Conclude

Complete this:

```text
Do not conclude that...
```

Examples:

```text
Do not conclude that a person used AI based only on polish or style.
```

```text
Do not treat a company page as a direct personal statement by one executive.
```

```text
Do not treat a wartime provenance gap as proof of wrongdoing.
```

## 4. Provide Known Context

Tell the AI what you already know.

```text
Known provenance or workflow:
- 

Known uncertainty:
- 

Known gaps:
- 

Reviewer intuition, if any:
- 
```

## 5. Tell the AI the Output You Need

Recommended instruction:

```text
Return a Tracewright-style review map with source inventory, claim map, evidence cards, alternative explanations, review lanes, limitations, and next checks. Keep authorship/mediation, disclosure/provenance, and claim reliability separate.
```

## 6. After the First Output

The first AI output is a draft. Ask follow-up questions before relying on it.

Useful follow-ups:

```text
Show me the exact source excerpts behind the top three review flags.
```

```text
Separate factual reliability issues from authorship or AI-mediation issues.
```

```text
What would change your interpretation?
```

```text
List the strongest alternative explanations for each flagged issue.
```

```text
Which observations are weak signals and should not be overused?
```

```text
What should a qualified human expert check next?
```
