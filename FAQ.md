# FAQ

## Is Tracewright an AI detector?

No. Tracewright is a review map method. It does not decide whether something was written by AI.

## Can Tracewright tell me whether someone used AI?

No. It can surface review cues, evidence gaps, workflow uncertainty, source roles, and next checks. It should not be used to accuse or expose people.

## What is a review map?

A review map separates claims, source roles, evidence, uncertainty, alternative explanations, and follow-up actions so a human reviewer can reason more carefully.

## What are Evidence Cards?

Evidence Cards are observation cards. They tie an observation to source material, reasoning, alternatives, and a next action. They are not suspicion cards or accusation cards.

## Why does Tracewright avoid scores?

Scores encourage premature judgment. Tracewright uses review posture labels and evidence cards to show what should be inspected next.

## Can I upload private messages or unpublished materials?

Only if you understand the AI service's data handling, have the right to use the materials there, and have considered consent and harm. If uncertain, use redacted, anonymized, synthetic, or non-sensitive excerpts first.

## Can I use this at work?

You can use it for non-commercial internal learning or review preparation, subject to the license and your workplace rules. Commercial use or client-facing service use requires permission.

## Can I use it for academic review?

Yes, as a review aid. It can help inspect claims, methods, citations, limitations, overclaiming, and source support. It does not replace supervisors, reviewers, editors, or disciplinary expertise.

## Can I use it in legal, HR, disciplinary, or other high-impact decisions?

Do not rely on AI output alone. Tracewright can organize questions and evidence for qualified human review, but it is not a decision system.

## What does "commercial use requires permission" mean?

You may not sell, repackage, host, integrate, or use Tracewright-based tools, prompts, workflows, dashboards, or methodology in a commercial product or client-facing paid service without prior written permission.

## What should I do if I only have a hunch?

Record the hunch as reader intuition, separate from evidence. Then ask what source excerpts, claims, alternatives, or follow-up checks would support or weaken that intuition.

## What if my AI assistant gives a verdict anyway?

Ask it to revise the output as a review map: remove verdict language, preserve source excerpts, add alternative explanations, separate review lanes, and state what cannot be concluded.
