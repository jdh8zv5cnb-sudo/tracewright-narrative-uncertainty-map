# Misuse Examples

Tracewright is a review map, not a finding. These examples show unsafe uses and safer reframings.

## Accusing Someone Of AI Use

Unsafe:

> Did this person use AI?

Why unsafe:

Style cues are weak evidence. Polished, rough, fluent, or unusual writing can have many explanations.

Safer:

> Which parts of this text show source-grounded detail, mediation/polish cues, workflow uncertainty, or claims requiring follow-up, and what alternative explanations remain plausible?

## Treating Polish As Proof

Unsafe:

> This is smooth, so it must be AI-generated.

Why unsafe:

Human editing, translation, professional style, templates, and careful revision can all create a smooth surface.

Safer:

> What parts of the surface seem highly mediated, and what workflow evidence would clarify whether editing, translation, AI assistance, or human revision shaped them?

## Treating Roughness As Proof

Unsafe:

> This is rough, so it must be human-written.

Why unsafe:

AI output can be prompted to imitate roughness. Human writing can also be edited by tools while preserving irregular phrasing.

Safer:

> What source-grounded details, continuity cues, or local judgments are visible, and how strong are they as review cues?

## Ranking People By Writing Texture

Unsafe:

> Rank these people from most human-like to least human-like.

Why unsafe:

That turns a review protocol into a classifier and can harm people.

Safer:

> Identify which materials need closer review for source grounding, claim support, disclosed workflow, or mediation/polish uncertainty.

## Using Evidence Cards As Accusation Cards

Unsafe:

> Use these Evidence Cards to list suspicious people.

Why unsafe:

Evidence Cards preserve observations and alternatives. They are not disciplinary labels.

Safer:

> Use Evidence Cards to identify what a human reviewer should inspect next and what cannot yet be concluded.

## High-Impact Decisions Without Human Review

Unsafe:

> Use this dashboard to decide whether to discipline a student, employee, author, vendor, or researcher.

Why unsafe:

AI-assisted review can be wrong or incomplete, and high-impact contexts require accountable human judgment.

Safer:

> Use Tracewright to prepare questions, source checks, and uncertainty notes for qualified human review.

## Uploading Private Or Unpublished Materials Without Consent

Unsafe:

> Upload private letters, drafts, client records, or unpublished manuscripts to a public or consumer AI system without checking consent or data handling.

Why unsafe:

The privacy risk may be greater than the review benefit.

Safer:

> Use redacted, anonymized, synthetic, or non-sensitive excerpts first. Confirm the AI environment is appropriate before uploading sensitive materials.

## Collapsing Different Source Roles Into One Voice

Unsafe:

> Treat company statements, third-party reporting, legal documents, personal posts, and later interpretation as if they all speak with one voice.

Why unsafe:

Different source roles support different claims. Collapsing them creates false consistency or false contradiction.

Safer:

> Preserve source roles and ask what each source can and cannot support.

## Treating Provenance Gaps As Proof Of Wrongdoing

Unsafe:

> There is a gap in the record, so wrongdoing occurred.

Why unsafe:

Gaps can result from lost records, archive limits, migration, war, institutional transfer, or incomplete review.

Safer:

> Name the gap, identify the strongest pre-gap and post-gap anchors, list plausible explanations, and define the next archival check.
